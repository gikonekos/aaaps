## Article 1 (Purpose)

This specification was created based on lessons learned from the 2002 "Takara incident" and the 2005 "Nomaneko controversy" in Japan. Both incidents involved works of character art and ASCII art — nurtured as shared cultural property on the Japanese internet — that came close to being made the subject of exclusive rights claims (including trademark registration) by a specific company or organization. These were not isolated events, but examples of a structural risk that can recur.

Based on this lesson, this specification aims to permanently guarantee free secondary use of the Covered Content (defined below), while preventing the shared parts of the community's culture from being enclosed by any single party.

## Article 2 (Covered Content and Scope of the Rights Claimed)

1. This specification covers the name "Gikoneko," derivative characters, illustrations, and merchandise designs created as Gikoneko, and related CGI source code, development images, and digital logs (collectively, the "Covered Content").
2. MOTOI Kenkichi (Gikoneko) is not the original author of "Gikoneko-neko" (the base AA character) itself, from which the Covered Content originates. This specification does not assert the rights of that original author; it is limited to the scope that MOTOI Kenkichi has personally created and preserved.

## Article 3 (Freedom of Use)

1. Users may reproduce, adapt, modify, and otherwise make secondary use of the Covered Content on a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free basis. No prior permission or notice is required.
2. When engaging in commercial use, merchandising, or large-scale redistribution, please provide the credit described in Article 4.
3. This project and its community do not consent to, and object to, any attempt by a third party to obtain exclusive intellectual property rights (such as trademark registration or an exclusive copyright claim) over the Covered Content or its derivatives. However, this specification does not have the legal effect of prohibiting such filings by third parties; rather, it is intended to serve as supporting material demonstrating facts relevant to public recognition or prior use, for example in an opposition or invalidation proceeding against such a filing.

## Article 5 (Maintaining Copyleft)

If you redistribute or publish a derivative work created by modifying Covered Content under this specification, it is strongly recommended (SHOULD) that you carry it forward under a license that likewise permits free modification and redistribution (copyleft). Open licenses such as Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0 and the GFDL are treated as satisfying this recommendation.

*Whether this Article should be a MUST (mandatory) or remain a SHOULD (recommended) requirement is an open issue. See GOVERNANCE.md (Japanese only) for details.*

## Article 6 (Relationship to CC BY-SA, etc.)

This specification is designed with reference to the spirit of open licenses such as [Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/deed.en) and the [GFDL](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/fdl-1.3.html), and with respect for ease of use alongside them. However, the formal legal relationship between this specification and CC BY-SA, etc. (including whether this specification could constitute an impermissible "additional restriction" under CC BY-SA) has not been conclusively determined as of this version, and remains an open issue to be revisited in light of professional advice.
