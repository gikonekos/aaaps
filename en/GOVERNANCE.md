# GOVERNANCE

> **This repository is maintained primarily in Japanese.**
> The Japanese document ([GOVERNANCE.md](../GOVERNANCE.md)) is the authoritative text. This English document is a reference translation provided for international readers and may not always be fully up to date with the Japanese original.

This document defines how the "#AAAP" project is operated and published. For the license terms themselves, see [AAAPS-v1.md](../AAAPS-v1.md) (Japanese) / [en/AAAPS-v1.md](./AAAPS-v1.md) (English reference translation).

## Division of roles between publication venues

| Venue | Role |
|---|---|
| The official "#AAAP" Facebook group/page | The community's home base. Announcements, discussion, and building a track record of public recognition. |
| The GitHub repository `aaaps` | The canonical archive. Commit history allows revision dates to be verified externally. |

The two are complementary: because revision history is hard to verify externally on Facebook alone, GitHub is used together with it as the canonical record of primary source material.

## The role of AI review

This project drew on review from multiple generative AI systems as part of its drafting process. The final content and the decision to publish are made by the editor, MOTOI Kenkichi.

## Revision process

1. Proposed revisions are raised as Issues or Pull Requests on GitHub.
2. Final adoption is decided by MOTOI Kenkichi (Gikoneko).
3. Once a version is finalized, it is given a version number in the filename (e.g. `AAAPS-v1.md`), and older versions are retained rather than deleted.

## Open issues

As of this version, the following points have not been conclusively resolved, even after review by multiple AI systems (Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, Gemini). They are explicitly marked as "open" in this public version and are expected to be revisited in light of future operational experience and professional (patent attorney / lawyer) advice.

1. **AAAPS Article 5 (whether copyleft carry-forward is mandatory)**: whether derivative works must (MUST) be carried forward under a copyleft-compatible license, or whether this should remain a strong recommendation (SHOULD). The current version adopts SHOULD, but this may be revisited depending on future operational experience.
2. **AAAPS Article 6 (legal relationship to CC BY-SA, etc.)**: whether this specification should be positioned as building on top of CC BY-SA, or merely as referencing it, affects the legal analysis (e.g. whether it could be considered an impermissible "additional restriction"). The current version takes the more cautious position (reference / respect only).

Because these go to the core of the license, they are not settled by AI review alone; they are left open on the premise that the public draft will continue to be refined over time.

## Disclaimer

This governance document defines operational procedures; it does not define the rights and obligations of users. For rights and obligations, [AAAPS-v1.md](../AAAPS-v1.md) governs.
